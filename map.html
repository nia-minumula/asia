02 / Geography

Where the community actually is.

Two layers you can toggle on and off: cultural centers and museums, and the city's ethnic enclaves — Chinatown, Asia on Argyle, and the Devon Avenue corridor together. Markers are placed for orientation — always confirm an exact address before visiting.

  • Chinese American Museum of ChicagoExhibits on Chinese American life in the Midwest
  • Heritage Museum of Asian ArtPan-Asian art collection, Chinatown
  • Japanese American Service CommitteeCommunity & cultural center, Uptown
  • Indo-American CenterSouth Asian social services & culture, West Ridge
  • Indo-American Heritage MuseumSouth Asian American history, Devon corridor
  • Midwest Buddhist TempleSite of the annual Ginza Holiday Festival
  • Chinatown (Near South Side)Anchored since the early 1900s, along Wentworth Ave
  • Chinatown SquareShopping plaza & zodiac statues
  • Asia on Argyle / "New Chinatown"Pan-Asian & Vietnamese corridor, Uptown

  • West Ridge / Rogers Park edgeOverlapping South Asian & East African communities

Field Notes

These neighborhoods are living communities, not exhibits. Visit like a guest: support local businesses, and be mindful during religious or cultural events.
