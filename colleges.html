Where to actually study this.

A quick note on terms: Asian American Studies is an ethnic studies field — it looks at Asian diasporic communities within the United States, their history, labor, and politics. Asian Studies is an area studies field — it looks at countries and regions in Asia themselves. Several Chicagoland schools now teach both, sometimes combined under "Global Asian Studies."

Asian American & Global Asian Studies

ethnic studies focus
Near West Side

University of Illinois Chicago

BA in Global Asian Studies

The first program of its kind in the Midwest, combining area studies and ethnic studies to look at Asia and its diasporas together — including new immigration patterns and anti-Asian, anti-Black, and anti-Arab/Muslim racism. The major grew out of a student movement that began more than 30 years ago.

  • OffersBA major, minor
  • Housed inCollege of Liberal Arts & Sciences
  • Also home toAsian American Resource & Cultural Center, AANAPISI initiative
Evanston

Northwestern University

Asian American Studies Program

A dedicated Asian American Studies program, distinct from the university's separate Department of Asian Languages & Cultures — reflecting the field's split between ethnic studies and area studies.

  • OffersMinor / coursework
  • Related dept.Asian Languages & Cultures
Lincoln Park

DePaul University

Global Asian Studies Program

An interdisciplinary program in the College of Liberal Arts & Social Sciences drawing faculty from history, political science, art, religious studies, and gender studies to study Asia and Asian diasporic communities together.

  • OffersMinor
  • Housed inCollege of Liberal Arts & Social Sciences
Rogers Park

Loyola University Chicago

Department of Asian Studies

A full academic department offering coursework spanning East, South, and Southeast Asian history, politics, and culture, with room to focus on diasporic and Asian American topics.

  • OffersMajor / minor

Asian Studies (Area Studies)

region & language focus — still worth knowing about
Hyde Park

University of Chicago

East Asian & South Asian Languages and Civilizations

Several linked departments and centers — East Asian Languages and Civilizations, the Center for East Asian Studies, and South Asian Languages and Civilizations — offer deep language and area-studies training, more focused on Asia than the Asian American diaspora specifically.

  • OffersMajor, minor, language sequences
Lake Forest

Lake Forest College

Asian Studies Program

A North Shore liberal arts college with a dedicated interdisciplinary Asian Studies program covering history, politics, and culture across the region.

  • OffersMajor / minor
Naperville

North Central College

East Asian Studies Program

A western-suburb liberal arts college offering an East Asian Studies track focused primarily on China, Japan, and Korea.

  • OffersMinor / concentration
DeKalb

Northern Illinois University

Center for Southeast Asian Studies

One of the country's federally recognized National Resource Centers for Southeast Asia, with strengths in Southeast Asian languages that feed directly into Chicago's Vietnamese, Cambodian, Lao, and Filipino communities.

  • OffersCertificate, language programs, grad study
Not enrolled anywhere yet? Several of these campuses welcome community members at public lectures and cultural events — check the Events page, or look for a "Global Asian Studies" or "Asian American Resource Center" contact on any campus website to ask about auditing a public talk.
